# RemyNest Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 5 June 2026
**Last updated:** 5 June 2026

RemyNest ("**RemyNest**", "**we**", "**us**", "**our**") provides an AI-powered memory
preservation platform that lets individuals, families and caregivers store, organise,
search and share personal memories, notes, photos and videos. This Privacy Policy
explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, the legal bases on which
we rely, how long we keep it, who we share it with, how we protect it, and the rights
you have. It applies to the RemyNest website at https://www.remynest.com, our iOS and
Android applications, and related services (together, the "**Service**").

This Policy is designed to meet the requirements of the EU General Data Protection
Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, the Irish Data Protection
Act 2018, and applicable privacy laws in the United States, Canada (PIPEDA) and
Australia (Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles).

## 2. The personal data we collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

**a) Account and identity data.** Your name and email address and authentication
identifiers. RemyNest uses **email/password sign-in only**; we do not offer or use any
third-party / social login (no Google or Apple sign-in).

**b) Authentication and credentials.** A securely hashed password (we never store
passwords in plain text). We do not receive or store any third-party login credentials.

**c) User-generated content.** Memories, notes, text, photos, videos and associated
metadata that you create or upload; in future, voice recordings and voice
transcriptions. This content may contain personal data about you and about other people
you choose to include. **It may reveal sensitive information** (for example, references
to health, family relationships or religious or philosophical life events). We do not
require, and we discourage you from providing, special-category data; where you choose
to include it, we process it on the basis described in Section 4.

**c) Family, sharing and caregiver data.** Information about people you invite or who
invite you (for example, an invitee email address), relationship roles, and the access
permissions you configure.

**d) Device and technical data.** Device type, operating system and version, app
version, language settings, IP address, and similar technical identifiers necessary to
operate and secure the Service.

**e) Log and diagnostic data.** Service logs, crash reports and error reports
(including stack traces and the technical context of an error) used to detect, diagnose
and fix problems and to protect the Service.

**f) Notification data.** Push notification tokens and your notification preferences,
used to deliver reminders and alerts you have enabled.

**g) Usage and analytics data.** If and where enabled (see Section 6 on Cookies and
Section 5 on AI), aggregated or pseudonymous information about how the Service is used,
such as feature interactions and session counts.

**h) Billing data (subscriptions).** If you purchase a subscription, our payment
processor collects and processes your payment details. We do not store full card
numbers; we retain limited records such as plan, status, and transaction identifiers.

We do **not** sell your personal data, and we do **not** use your content to build
advertising profiles.

## 3. Why we collect it (purposes)

We use personal data to:

1. create and administer your account and authenticate you;
2. provide the core Service — storing, organising, searching, displaying and sharing
   your memories, notes, photos and videos;
3. provide AI features that categorise, organise, summarise and make your content
   searchable (see Section 5);
4. deliver notifications and reminders you have enabled;
5. operate, secure, monitor, debug and improve the Service, including error and crash
   diagnostics;
6. process subscriptions, billing and related records;
7. communicate with you about service, security and administrative matters, and (only
   with your consent where required) product updates;
8. comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service; and
9. detect, prevent and address fraud, abuse and security incidents.

## 4. Legal bases for processing (GDPR / UK GDPR)

We rely on the following legal bases under Article 6 GDPR:

| Purpose | Legal basis |
|---|---|
| Creating/administering your account; providing the core Service; AI features that deliver the product you requested | **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) — necessary to perform our agreement with you |
| Security, fraud prevention, debugging, error/crash diagnostics, service improvement | **Legitimate interests** (Art. 6(1)(f)) — to keep the Service safe, reliable and functional, balanced against your rights |
| Optional analytics and non-essential cookies; optional product marketing | **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)) — you may withdraw at any time |
| Billing, tax and accounting records; responding to lawful requests | **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)) |
| Subscriptions/payments | **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) |

**Special-category data.** Where content you choose to upload reveals special-category
data (e.g., health or religious life events) within the meaning of Article 9 GDPR, we
process it only on the basis of your **explicit consent** (Art. 9(2)(a)), which you
provide by knowingly choosing to include such content, and you may withdraw it by
editing or deleting that content or your account. RemyNest does not use such content
to make any decision about you and does not process it for any purpose other than
providing the Service to you.

You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing
before withdrawal. Where we rely on legitimate interests, you have the right to object
(see Section 10).

## 5. Artificial intelligence (AI) processing

RemyNest uses artificial intelligence to enhance your experience. Specifically, AI is
used to:

- categorise and organise your memories and notes;
- generate summaries of content you have created;
- improve search and retrieval across your content; and
- assist content organisation features within the Service.

**How AI processing works.** To provide these features, relevant content you submit
(for example, the text of a note, or metadata about a memory) is processed by our AI
sub-processor, **OpenAI**, through its application programming interfaces. This
processing is carried out under a data processing agreement and, where applicable,
appropriate international transfer safeguards (see Section 9).

**What our AI does NOT do.** RemyNest's AI is an organisational and productivity tool.
It is **not** a medical device and does **not** provide medical advice, diagnosis,
clinical assessment, treatment recommendations, or cognitive or health evaluations.
AI-generated summaries and categorisations may contain errors and must not be relied
upon for any medical, legal, financial or other professional decision. RemyNest does
not use AI to make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about
you, and you are not subject to solely automated decision-making within the meaning of
Article 22 GDPR.

**Model training.** We do not permit your content to be used to train third-party
foundation models. Our AI sub-processor is engaged on terms that prohibit use of API
content to train its models.

You can use most of the Service without AI features by not invoking them; some
organisational conveniences may be unavailable if AI processing is disabled.

On our website we use:

  and keep the Service secure. These are always on and do not require consent.
- **Functional cookies** that remember your preferences.
- **Analytics cookies** (e.g., Google Analytics and/or PostHog), used only **with your

Where required by the ePrivacy Directive/Regulations (EU/UK), non-essential cookies are
set only after you consent through our cookie banner, and you can change your choices

advertising SDKs and do not track you across other companies' apps or websites. See our
separate **Cookie Policy** for the full cookie inventory.

## 7. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes set out above:

| Data | Retention |
|---|---|
| Account data and user content | For the life of your account, until you delete the content or your account |
| Backups containing your data | Purged on a rolling cycle; backup copies are deleted within **30 days** of deletion from the live system |
| Logs and diagnostic/error data | Up to **90 days**, then deleted or aggregated |
| Notification tokens | Until you disable notifications, revoke the token, or delete your account |
| Billing/tax records | As required by law (typically **6–7 years**) |
| DSAR and consent records | As required to demonstrate compliance (typically up to **6 years**) |

When you delete your account, we delete or irreversibly anonymise your personal data
in accordance with our **Data Deletion Policy** (`03-data-deletion-policy.md`), subject
to limited retention required by law. Two consequences of our shared, caregiver-based
model are worth highlighting:

- **Shared care profiles you own are transferred, not destroyed.** If you delete your
  account while owning a care profile that other accepted caregivers use, ownership is
  transferred to another accepted caregiver so the shared record survives; if none
  exists, the profile is deleted with your account.
- **Memories you contributed to other people's profiles are retained in anonymised
  form** (your authorship is removed and shown as a "Deleted Contributor"), preserving
  the other person's record, **unless you choose to delete them** during the deletion
  flow. We never re-attribute your entries to another real person.

## 8. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

- **Access** the personal data we hold about you and obtain a copy;
- **Rectify** inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **Erase** your data ("right to be forgotten");
- **Restrict** or **object to** processing (including processing based on legitimate
  interests, and any direct marketing);
- **Data portability** — receive your data in a structured, commonly used,
  machine-readable format and, where technically feasible, have it transmitted to
  another controller;
- **Withdraw consent** at any time where processing is based on consent; and
- **Lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority.

RemyNest provides self-service tools in the app to **export your data** and to **delete
your account**. You may also exercise any right by emailing **privacy@remynest.com** or
**dpo@remynest.com**. We will respond within **one month** (extendable by two further
months for complex requests, with notice). We do not charge a fee unless a request is
manifestly unfounded or excessive.

**Region-specific rights.**
- **EU/EEA & UK:** the rights above. You may complain to the Irish Data Protection
  Commission (dataprotection.ie) or your local EU authority, or, in the UK, the ICO
  (ico.org.uk).
- **United States (incl. California/CPRA and similar state laws):** rights to know,
  access, delete, correct, and to opt out of "sale"/"sharing" of personal information.
  **RemyNest does not sell or share personal information** as those terms are defined,
  and does not process sensitive personal information for purposes requiring an opt-out.
  We do not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.
- **Canada (PIPEDA):** rights of access and correction; you may complain to the Office
  of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.
- **Australia (Privacy Act / APPs):** rights of access and correction; you may complain
  to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

## 11. Children's privacy

RemyNest is **not directed to children under 16** and we do not knowingly collect
personal data from children under 16 without verifiable parental/guardian consent. The
apps are rated to restrict download by young children. If you are a parent or guardian
and believe a child has provided us personal data, contact **privacy@remynest.com** and
we will delete it. Where an adult account holder chooses to include a child's
information within their own memories, that adult is responsible for having the
authority to do so.

## 12. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal
data, including:

- **Encryption in transit** (TLS) and **encryption at rest** for stored data and media;
- **Row-Level Security** and access controls that isolate each account's data, enforced
  at the database layer;
- the principle of **least privilege** for staff and service access, with
  authentication and audit logging;
- secure, access-controlled cloud storage for media;
- **secret management** and protected administrative endpoints;
- continuous monitoring and **error/crash tracking** to detect and remediate issues; and
- regular **backups** with a defined retention and deletion cycle.

No system is perfectly secure. In the event of a personal data breach likely to result
in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent supervisory
authority within **72 hours** where required, and affected users without undue delay
where the breach is likely to result in a high risk.
